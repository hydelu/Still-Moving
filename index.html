Still. Moving.

When still photography meets moving image.

This is a personal project inspired by “24 Frames” - the experimental film directed by eminent Iranian filmmaker Abbas Kiarostami.

More From YouTube

“24 Frames” is a collection of 24 short four-and-a-half minute films which contains still images, paintings and photographs selected by Kiarostami. The film premiered at the 70th Cannes Film Festival in 2017 after his death in July 2016.

Watching “24 Frames” is an unique movie-watching experience I’ve never had before. It’s more like a refined meditation to immerse yourself, in the stark landscapes with wild lives, or just behind windows staring outside world. The still photographs and moving images are no longer separated, they become one whole in harmony.

Expression is full of complications and mystery because that’s my perception of life.

- Abbas Kiarostami

The Director

Abbas Kiarostami (22 June 1940 – 4 July 2016) was an Iranian film director, screenwriter, poet, photographer, and film producer. He attained critical acclaim for directing the Koker trilogy (1987–1994), Close-Up (1990), The Wind Will Carry Us (1999), and Taste of Cherry (1997), which was awarded the Palme d’Or at the Cannes Film Festival that year.

Still.

Moving.


“24 Frames” really inspired me to think about the relationship between still photography (as single frame) and moving image (as multiple sequential frames). What are the possibilities can be created when combing them into one form? What’s more the audience could see and feel from this composition?


Kiarostami spent five years using advanced CG technology to accomplish this masterpiece. I choose to adopt cinemagraph since it’s more realistic for this project. The final work comprises 5 cinemagraphs, which contain different themes from living world to classic movie, from reality to supernatural. I tried to use different techniques to create new contexts and watching experiences for people's own interpretation.



Now use imagination to see and to create your own stories.
